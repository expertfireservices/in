Director of Industrial Safety and Health (DISH)

1) Plan Approvals from the DISH Office:


Preparation of Safety Plans: Develop and prepare various safety plans required by the DISH office for approval, such as:
  • Safety Program Plans: Comprehensive safety protocols for a specific work environment.
  • Emergency Action Plans (EAP): Plans outlining the procedures to follow in case of emergencies (e.g., fire, chemical spill, natural disaster).
  • Hazardous Materials Handling Plans: Plans for safely managing hazardous materials, including storage, handling, disposal, and emergency responses.
  • Ergonomics Plans: Programs to mitigate risks associated with repetitive stress, physical strain, and poor workstation design.
  • Workplace Injury Prevention Plans: Proactive strategies for reducing injuries, identifying hazards, and maintaining compliance with industry safety standards.
Plan Submission: Submit the safety plans to the DISH office for approval, ensuring the documents meet regulatory standards and are fully compliant.
Revisions and Updates: In cases where plans are rejected or require modifications, assist with revising and resubmitting the necessary documentation to ensure approval.
Compliance with Regulations: Ensure that the submitted plans meet all relevant safety standards and legal requirements, such as OSHA, local workplace safety laws, and specific industry standards.

2) Documentation and Form Preparation:


Required Forms for Plan Approval and Renewal:
  • Safety Program Documentation: Detailed records of the company’s safety program, training records, hazard analysis, and risk assessments.
  • DISH Application Forms: Forms required for license renewal and the submission of various safety plans.
  • Incident Reports: Documentation of any safety incidents that have occurred during the previous licensing period.
  • Inspection and Audit Forms: Detailed records of safety audits and inspections conducted within the company, demonstrating compliance with safety standards.
  • OSHA and Other Regulatory Forms: Depending on the jurisdiction, forms like the OSHA 300 Log or other records for injury reporting may be necessary.
  • Certification of Compliance: Statements or forms that confirm compliance with safety and health regulations, required for both plan approval and license renewal.

3) Regulatory and Legal Consultation:


  • Appeals and Disputes: Assist businesses with appealing rejected plans or navigating disputes with regulatory authorities regarding license renewals or plan approvals.
  • Audit and Inspection Preparation: Prepare for scheduled or surprise audits by the DISH office, including conducting mock audits, reviewing safety policies, and making recommendations to correct deficiencies before the official inspection.
  • Incident Reporting and Documentation: Guide companies in accurately reporting any incidents that might affect their safety record or approval process, ensuring all necessary forms and documentation are filed correctly.
  • Regulatory Interpretation: Help businesses interpret and understand complex regulations from the DISH office, OSHA, or other regulatory bodies, ensuring that their operations align with legal requirements.

4) Handling Special Safety Approvals:


  • High-Risk Operations: If the business involves high-risk activities (e.g., working with heavy machinery, chemicals, or hazardous waste), the DISH consultant ensures that specialized plans are submitted for approval, addressing those risks.
  • Facility-Specific Safety Approvals: Assist with obtaining necessary approvals for safety measures specific to the company’s facility (e.g., fire safety, chemical storage, ventilation systems).
  • Temporary or Seasonal Work Plans: For businesses that operate under temporary conditions or seasonal operations, the consultant prepares the appropriate safety plans for DISH approval.

Forms Typically Involved in the Process:

  • DISH License Application Form: This is the core form to apply for a new or renewed DISH license.
  • Safety Plan Submission Forms: Forms used to submit various types of safety plans to the DISH office for approval (e.g., hazard analysis, emergency plans).
  • Inspection and Compliance Reports: Forms for documenting safety inspections, compliance audits, and the status of safety measures.
  • Accident and Incident Reports: These are forms to be filed after any workplace accidents or injuries, and they may be required during the license renewal process.
  • Incident Investigation Forms: Documents used to investigate and report on workplace accidents or safety violations.
  • Safety Training Records and Certification: Forms that document employee training on safety protocols, machine operation, PPE use, etc.
  • Emergency Action Plan Approval Forms: These are required when submitting emergency action plans for approval.
  • Hazard Communication Plan Forms: Required forms to comply with hazard communication regulations, especially when dealing with chemicals or toxic substances in the workplace.
